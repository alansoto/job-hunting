# Xero Performance Review — Alan Soto (2026-06)

Source: provided verbatim by Alan. Ingested 2026-06-19.

Alan is a Technical Program Manager currently operating at a "Lead" level, who has demonstrated
significant value in execution-heavy environments but is currently facing challenges regarding
strategic expectation alignment and communication at the leadership level.

## Strengths

- **Ownership and Accountability:** Alan demonstrates high ownership, consistently leaning into
  challenging problem sets and taking responsibility for workstreams, such as the practice tools
  program and reporting artifacts. He is noted for his drive to do the right thing and for
  following through on feedback.
- **Facilitation and Process Optimization:** He is a strong optimizer of team processes. He excels
  at taking existing team workflows and improving them through tools like Jira, story mapping, and
  retrospectives, which improves cycle time.
- **Emotional Intelligence (EQ):** Alan is widely regarded as a personable and optimistic team
  member. His ability to build relationships and break down walls in collaborative settings is a
  "force multiplier" for his work, making him highly pleasant to work with.
- **Execution in Structured Environments:** He successfully navigated and landed impacts in defined
  problem sets, particularly within the practice tools and AV programs, where he effectively
  centralized information and created sources of truth.
- **Adaptability:** He shows a willingness to lean into new, difficult areas (like AI and API
  modernization) despite the ambiguity and challenges associated with them.

## Areas for Improvement

- **Technical Fluency:** While Alan has made a concerted effort to increase his technical knowledge
  (e.g., using AI tools and creating visualizations), he needs to further his understanding of
  complex technical alignment at the ecosystem level to better guide direction, rather than just
  focusing on lower-level technical details.
- **Executive Communication and Clarity:** Stakeholders have noted that his communication,
  particularly in verbal summaries during high-stakes meetings (like weekly strategy reviews), can
  be unfocused. He needs to develop the ability to distill information into high-density, concise
  points, avoiding the need for stakeholders to do "heavy lifting" to understand the status or the
  path forward.
