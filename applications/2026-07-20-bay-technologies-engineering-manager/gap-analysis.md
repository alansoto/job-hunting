## Fit score — Moderate-to-strong

The leadership shape is a clean, repeated match: leading and coaching a small multidisciplinary team,
Agile delivery, PR/code-review/CI-CD discipline, architecture guidance, technical credibility, and —
unusually valuable here — **genuine AI-first engineering evidence** (AI for Customers, NLP analytics,
daily AI coding tools, Nagare built with Claude Code). That AI-first alignment plus the
software-engineer-to-leader arc is what carries the application.

The friction is real and concentrated in one place: the JD prescribes a **specific hands-on stack
(.NET/C#, SQL Server, JS/TS, Python)** and Azure, and expects occasional production-code
contribution. .NET/C# and Python are genuine, unbridgeable gaps; TS is AI-assisted-only; Azure and
Git-hosting platforms are standing gaps softened by the strongly-evidenced CI/CD/DevOps *practice*.
Because the JD frames the technical asks as *leadership across* a stack plus *credibility to guide
and coach* (with hands-on coding only "from time to time"), the gaps are contextualisable rather than
disqualifying — but they must be handled with the honest capability-hierarchy / fast-ramp framing,
not glossed over. Net: a strong people-and-AI-leadership fit with an honest, plainly-acknowledged
stack gap. Worth applying; Format B (narrative), headline "Engineering Manager."
