Engineering Manager, Squad-Based Delivery

This position is based in Brisbane, Australia.

What We Offer

This is the central leadership role in Opengear’s squad-based engineering model. You’ll lead 6-9 engineers across 2-3 domain squads—owning their delivery outcomes, backlog quality, and career development—while building the self-organising, Kanban-oriented culture that will define how Opengear delivers software for years to come. This is a leadership role that asks you to step back from day-to-day technical contribution and invest your focus where it matters most: coaching your teams, preparing high-quality work, and removing everything that stands between your squads and uninterrupted delivery.

What You Will Do

    Own delivery outcomes for 2-3 squads of 3–4 engineers each, managing capacity, domain coverage, and backlog quality across both feature delivery and support rotation cycles
    Lead feature refinement from initial requirements to squad-ready stories—working collaboratively with SMEs within your squads to ensure work is well-scoped, correctly sized, and genuinely ready before it enters the queue
    Coach squads toward self-organization: running ceremonies autonomously, self-diagnosing impediments, and owning their own continuous improvement without requiring the EM to facilitate
    Manage support rotation scheduling for your squads, ensuring a prepared backlog of feature work is ready for squads returning from rotation so delivery resumes immediately
    Build domain depth and cross-skilling across your squads over time, identifying knowledge gaps and developing structured plans to close them
    Conduct regular 1:1s, set and track clear goals, and actively develop each engineer’s career—including identifying and preparing engineers for tech lead responsibilities
    Collaborate with the Solutions PO, product stakeholders, and peer EMs on feature planning, priority alignment, and cross-squad dependencies

 

Who You Are and What You Bring

    Engineering manager with a demonstrated track record of leading small, high-performing delivery teams
    Comfortable leading without being in every meeting—you build team autonomy and self-sufficiency, not dependency on your presence
    Strong instinct for backlog quality and feature refinement; you know what ‘squad-ready’ looks like and can build collaborative processes to get there reliably
    Familiar with Kanban or flow-based delivery models; you understand throughput, cycle time, and queue health, and you use them to manage delivery—not just report on it
    Technically credible enough to assess work complexity, challenge sizing, and coach engineers through implementation trade-offs
    Proven record of developing engineers toward senior and tech lead capability
    Clear communicator who can work across product, engineering, and support contexts without losing signal

 

Desired But Not Required

    Familiarity with Opengear’s product domains (Lighthouse, NGCS, OGCS, OEM)
    Experience facilitating retrospectives and driving measurable improvement in team practices
    Exposure to flow metrics: cycle time, throughput, queue depth, and delivery forecasting
