# JD Analysis — Opengear, Engineering Manager, Squad-Based Delivery

**Primary pain point.** Their squads stall. The JD names the failure modes precisely: work entering the queue before it is ready, squads that need the EM to facilitate their own ceremonies, and delivery that does not resume after a support rotation because nothing was prepared. They are hiring someone to remove the EM as a bottleneck, not to add a layer.

**Top 3 priorities.** (1) Squad self-sufficiency - stated twice, once in the intro and again as a "Who You Are" bullet ("not dependency on your presence"). (2) Backlog quality and refinement to a "squad-ready" bar. (3) Kanban flow managed by metrics, with the pointed qualifier "not just report on it". People development (1:1s, tech-lead pipeline) is a strong fourth.

**Culture signals.** Measured, deliberate, self-aware. They have thought hard about their own model and are naming where it breaks. No scale-up energy, no emoji, no growth-rocket language, despite Digi being a global company. The line "invest your focus where it matters most" reads like a team that has watched EMs spend focus in the wrong places.

**Best hook angle.** "Comfortable leading without being in every meeting." Alan spent four years at Xero coaching EMs and tech leads across 10+ teams while owning none of them - influence without authority is the whole shape of that role, and the performance review's "force multiplier" and "strong optimizer of team processes" language is third-party proof. Pair it with Go1 for the direct people ownership the JD leads with. Closest reference letter: Karmo (2026-07-09), same role shape - but do not reuse its scale-up-preference opener, the tone is wrong for Opengear.

**Gap trigger.** Yes, but light. Networking / out-of-band infrastructure (Lighthouse, NGCS, OGCS, OEM) is flagged "Desired But Not Required". One short paragraph, bridged via Suncorp's telephony platform replacement and the software-engineer background. Nothing else in the JD needs acknowledging - no named tool, framework, or certification is required, which is a first among recent applications.
