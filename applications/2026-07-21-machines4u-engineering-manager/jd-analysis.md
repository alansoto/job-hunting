# JD Analysis - Machines4U (carsales), Engineering Manager

**Primary pain point.** Machines4U is a smaller business sitting inside a much larger group. It runs a high-availability product that dealers depend on daily, on a stack the ad calls "mixed legacy and modern", and carsales wants one person who can own engineering end to end: keep it stable, migrate it toward group-aligned systems, and do that without a rewrite and without dropping commercial pace. The word repeated across the duties is "pragmatic".

**Top 3 priorities.** (1) Own engineering execution and turn business priorities into a sequenced roadmap that actually gets delivered. (2) Lead the progressive modernisation, balancing legacy stability against migration to modern group systems. (3) Lead a distributed team and hold both the technical and behavioural bar, while being credible with senior leadership on risk, trade-offs, and capacity.

**Culture signals.** "We strive to feel small, small enough to stay agile" - they want scale-up behaviour inside a corporate group. Warm, benefits-forward, explicitly inclusive, and the ad closes by telling candidates who do not meet every requirement to apply anyway. Confident-but-human tone; not a place for corporate stiffness, and not a place for scale-up brashness either.

**Best hook angle.** The Xero API Program modernisation. Retiring a legacy API surface across dozens of teams while millions of customers kept using the products, proved with the smallest possible slice and de-risked with a 1% traffic test before the real cutover. That is the JD's hardest and most specific ask answered almost verbatim, and it pairs naturally with the executive-facing sequencing view that followed. Closest precedent in [[cover-letter-examples]] is the Bay Technologies letter (modernising a mature portfolio) and the Opengear/Randstad Digital "name the unglamorous reality" framing.

**Gap trigger.** Yes - one. "Strong technical depth across modern web stacks (backend and frontend)". The honest position is architecture-level judgement plus AI-assisted building, not practitioner fluency, and the last decade has been leading rather than shipping. The JD's own phrasing ("hands-on enough to review architecture, unblock engineers, and spot quality problems early") is a lower bar than proficiency, so raise it once, plainly, and land on what he does bring. Do not raise CRM, marketing platforms, budget, or the machinery domain.
