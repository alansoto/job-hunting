# Gap Analysis - Davidson / ICT Project Manager (Fleet Technology Transformation)

**Date:** 2026-07-20
**Role:** ICT Project Manager, 12-month contract, $111ph + super
**Recruiter:** Davidson. End client not named; the vocabulary ("large and diverse fleet", "corporate
stakeholders", "procurement", "governance groups", government experience "highly regarded") reads
like a council, a state government agency, or a utility.

---

## What this role actually is

A **project establishment role**, not a run-the-steady-state role. The JD is explicit: "establish the
foundations for a new enterprise technology capability". The 12 months cover governance setup ->
requirements -> market engagement -> PoC/pilot -> implementation planning. It stops before full
rollout. That shape matters, because Alan's strongest and most repeated pattern is exactly this:
joining at the ambiguous front end and building the governance, cadence, and reporting machinery
that makes the rest executable.

Fleet technology means telematics - in-vehicle devices reporting location, driver behaviour,
utilisation, and compliance data back to a central platform. That is an IoT/data-platform program
with a physical installation component.

Delivery and capacity forecasting are evidenced; formal budget ownership is not. Best available
bridge: 2sol, where Alan owned end-to-end delivery of a platform generating $5M+ per month, and Xero
roadmaps shaped within commercials. Frame as commercial ownership. **Recommendation: do not raise it
as a gap** - it is one word in a six-item list, and raising it manufactures doubt (same call as Lab3
and Randstad Digital).

**PoC installation, communications, training and support readiness.** The change-and-readiness side
is adjacent rather than direct: Xero's domain onboarding model and org-wide ways-of-working rollout
(training, coaching, shareback material), Virgin's tell-show-do-review coaching, intelliHR's pilot
feedback loops. What is genuinely absent is *physical* installation - fitting hardware into vehicles
and running a field rollout. Closest analogue in the wiki is Suncorp's enterprise telephony
replacement, which had physical endpoints across every brand. Moderate, and worth one line.

**Benefits realisation.** First JD in the wiki to name it. Not evidenced as a named discipline.

intelliHR, behavioural analytics (Pendo, FullStory) to prove adoption rather than assume it, and

has not run a formal benefits-realisation framework. Low-moderate severity - do not raise separately,
but do use outcome-measurement language in the resume so the capability is visible.

## Gaps

**1. Buy-side procurement and market engagement (issuing RFIs, vendor evaluation, procurement
planning).** *Substantially revised 2026-07-20 after Alan confirmed the 2sol / BMA tender.*

This is the third recurrence (Paxus 2026-07-07, Randstad Digital 2026-07-15), and the first time it
is a **headline duty with its own bullet in both The Role and About You**. In the prior two JDs it
was a single clause inside one duty and was correctly left unraised. That call does not hold here.

What is now evidenced - and this is stronger than the first pass of this analysis assumed:
- **A formal RFP/tender process managed end-to-end.** At 2sol, Alan won a competitive tender with
  **BMA (BHP Mitsubishi Alliance)** to build interactive mobile learning content for training
  dragline operators. He ran the entire tender process, then owned the client relationship,
  implementation, and delivery. This is the only formal tender evidence anywhere in the wiki.
- Vendor and contractor management: Suncorp (external vendors plus offshore contractors), Xero
  (cross-vendor coordination inside the API Program).
- Deep external supplier integration owned scope-to-release: Console/Reapit's payments provider.
- Solution evaluation and selection thinking: technical spikes, prototypes, and options assessment
  used repeatedly to choose a path before committing to build (Go1, Console/Reapit, intelliHR).

**The distinction that still matters.** The BMA tender was run from the **supply side** - Alan
responded to a client-issued RFP and won it. This JD asks for the **buy side**: issuing RFIs,
evaluating competing vendors, and planning procurement on behalf of the purchasing organisation.
Those are different seats at the same table. What genuinely transfers is real, though: he knows how a
tender process is structured and governed, what a submission has to contain, how evaluation criteria
are read and answered, and - usefully for an evaluator - what a supplier does to make a weak response
look strong. See the guardrail in [[experience]].

**Severity: moderate, downgraded from the initial read.** The JD places procurement *alongside* ICT
and business stakeholders, implying a procurement function to partner with rather than a process for
the PM to own solo. **Recommendation: still acknowledge the buy-side distinction plainly in the cover
letter** - it is too prominent a duty to pass over in silence, and the honest version is now a
genuinely strong answer rather than a deflection. On the resume, claim tender process management and
vendor engagement (both true); do **not** claim RFI issuance, vendor evaluation, or procurement
planning.

**3. Named PM frameworks (Prince2, MSP, PMBoK).**

## Fit score

**Strong.**

The core of this role - arriving at the front end of an ambiguous enterprise technology initiative
and building the governance, reporting, schedule, and stakeholder machinery that turns it into
something executable - is the single most repeated pattern in Alan's career, and Suncorp is a
near-literal precedent for it inside a large, regulated, compliance-critical organisation with
operational, infrastructure, security, business, and vendor stakeholders all in play.

Two things sit against it, and one of them shrank considerably during drafting. Procurement is
prominent enough here to require an honest sentence, but the 2sol / BMA tender means the honest
sentence is now "I have run a formal tender end-to-end, from the supplier's side of the table"
rather than "I have not done this". Fleet remains a genuinely new domain, though the JD never asks
for it. Neither is disqualifying, and both are the kind of gap this JD's own framing ("working
closely with ICT, operational, procurement and business stakeholders") anticipates being covered by
the surrounding team.

The contract shape also works in Alan's favour: he is available immediately, and a 12-month
establishment-phase contract is a good match for someone whose demonstrated strength is standing
things up rather than running them for years.
