# JD Analysis - Davidson / ICT Project Manager

**Primary pain point.** They are at the start of something and it has no shape yet. "Establish the
foundations for a new enterprise technology capability" is the giveaway. The risk they are hiring
against is a twelve-month contract that burns through governance setup and market engagement and
still arrives at the implementation decision without enough evidence to make it.

**Top 3 priorities.** (1) Governance, delivery controls, reporting and schedules, listed first in
both The Role and About You. (2) Coordinating business requirements across three distinct
stakeholder groups - operational, ICT, corporate. (3) Market engagement and procurement, which is
the only duty given its own bullet in both lists.

**Culture signals.** Measured, structured, procedural. "Governance groups", "decision-ready
recommendations", "benefits realisation", "corporate stakeholders" - this is public-sector or
utility vocabulary. No energy language, no product language. The reader wants rigour, not pace.

**Best hook angle.** Suncorp. An enterprise platform replacement inside a regulated ASX-listed
organisation where the governance did not exist and Alan built it, across four teams plus offshore
contractors, external vendors, and Infrastructure, Security, and Business units. It answers
priority (1) and priority (2) in one engagement. Reference pattern: the Randstad Digital letter
(2026-07-15) - soft recruiter-facing opener plus a paragraph naming the unglamorous reality of the
work, which is the confirmed win for recruiter-placed contract roles.

**Gap trigger.** Yes, two. Formal RFI and procurement process ownership is unevidenced and is a
headline duty here, unlike the last two JDs where it was one clause and correctly left unraised.
Fleet and telematics is a genuine new domain but the JD never asks for it - one plain sentence,
Karmo-style, no forced analogy. Budget and benefits realisation deliberately not raised.
