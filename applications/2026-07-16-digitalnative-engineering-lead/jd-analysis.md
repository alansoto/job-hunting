# JD Analysis — Engineering Lead (Digital Native)

**Primary pain point.** Read past the duty list and this is not a hiring need for raw engineering
throughput - it is a need for someone who develops engineers. The tell is the line "This isn't a
role for someone who wants to hoard the hard problems." That sentence is written from experience.
They have either had a technical lead who took every interesting problem and left the team junior,
or they are actively guarding against one. The hard problems are not the deliverable; the engineers
who can solve them next time are.

**Top 3 priorities.** (1) People development - mentoring, psychological safety, stretch
opportunities, direct feedback, "raises the bar through their own example". Three full paragraphs
versus one line on the stack. (2) True trio partnership with a PM and Designer, co-owning outcomes
rather than managing delivery. (3) Technical direction and modern engineering practice - CI/CD,
DevOps, SRE, automated testing, architecture latitude.

**Culture signals.** Measured, self-aware, values-forward. No emoji, no growth-rocket language, no
hype. The phrase "genuine people development" implies they have seen the fake kind. "Working on
problems that genuinely matter and have visible impact" plus "keeps major cities moving" says they
are proud of the mission and expect the reader to care about it. This is the Opengear tone, not the
Karmo tone - do not reach for the scale-up preference device or an exclamation mark.

**Best hook angle.** The anti-hoarding line, answered with the Practice-domain handover at Xero:
bootstrapped the subgraph, got UK Tax live as the first GA production query on the federated graph,
then deliberately handed the domain to the team that should own it with pair programming to make it
stick. Giving away the most interesting problem in the program is the literal answer to their
literal ask, and it is a named, specific, verifiable piece of work. Reference pattern:
the "When I read..." hook from the Stake letter, tightened to a senior direct-alignment opener.

**Gap trigger.** No. React/TypeScript proficiency is genuinely unmet (see gap-analysis) but Alan's
decision on 2026-07-16 is to downplay rather than acknowledge - no gap paragraph. Transport domain
is a nice-to-have and gets no paragraph either. The letter runs clean: hook, leadership evidence,
trio evidence, engineering practice, close.

**Format.** Prose, not bullets - senior role, and the JD's own register rewards judgement over
scannability. One page. Close with immediate availability per the standing positioning note.
