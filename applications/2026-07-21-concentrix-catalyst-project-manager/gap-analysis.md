## Gaps

### 1. Native Mobile - the one real gap. Moderate severity.
The JD names it as an expertise requirement: *"particularly in the Native Mobile space."*

Nothing in the wiki evidences native iOS/Android delivery. This is the **second recurrence** of the
mobile gap - Halosoft (2026-06-16) flagged `mobile app delivery` as preferred and unevidenced.

Bridges available, all partial:
- **2sol / BMA** - "interactive **mobile** learning content" for dragline operators. [[projects]]
  records an open question: *whether the "mobile" content was native app or web*. **Do not describe
  this as native mobile delivery** until that is confirmed. Safe as "mobile learning content".
- **2sol / Flight Centre** - 10+ customer-facing products in a consumer booking platform. Whether
  any were native apps is not recorded. Do not assume.
- Everything else in the wiki is web, platform, or data delivery.

Handling: acknowledge plainly in the cover letter using the **capability-hierarchy reframe**
([[positioning]]) - the platform is the learnable part, running a client's concurrent delivery is
what takes years. Do **not** claim native mobile experience on the resume. Do not stretch the BMA
project into a native mobile claim.

**Worth a prompt to Alan:** were any of the Flight Centre products native mobile apps, and was the
BMA content a native app or web? Either answer would close or shrink this gap materially.

### 2. Smartsheet - low severity.
Named alongside Jira and Confluence. Not evidenced; recurrence of the MS Project gap (Paxus,
2026-07-07). It is a scheduling/tracking tool in a list that is otherwise a clean match. Do not
claim it, do not raise it - the JD says "and other project tools", which signals tool-agnosticism.

### 3. Consultancy/agency as a current mode - not a gap, but note the recency.
The vendor-to-client evidence is 2sol (2012-2017) and Virgin via Neu21 (2019). The last six years
are in-house. Frame 2sol as formative rather than dated, and pair it with Xero's internal-client
pattern (domain teams as consumers of the API Program) to show the muscle is current.

### 4. Not raised deliberately.
Budget/financial forecasting (standing gap, unnamed in this JD), procurement, industry vertical
(Catalyst is sector-agnostic), and formal PM methodology acronyms (Master of Project Management +
PMP cover it). None are named here.

## Fit score: Strong

Nine of the ten duty and requirement lines are cleanly evidenced, and two of them - concurrent
projects for a single client, and being that client's single point of contact - are among the most
literal matches in the wiki, because 2sol was exactly that arrangement for five years. Kanban is

team leadership is a direct Suncorp match.

The application turns on how Native Mobile is handled. It is a stated expertise requirement and
Alan does not have it. Handled honestly with the capability-hierarchy reframe it reads as a
platform gap in an otherwise unusually close role-shape match; overclaimed via the BMA project it
becomes a credibility risk at interview. Take the honest route.

## Format decision

**Format B (narrative)** - the project default, and the right call here: this is a permanent senior
client-facing role where judgement and client-relationship narrative carry the pitch. Not a
keyword-screened contract.

Narrative cutline (today 2026-07-21, ~six years back = mid-2020): Xero (2022-2026) and Go1
(2020-2022) get the full narrative treatment. **Suncorp (Nov 2019 - Nov 2020) sits right on the
boundary** - same call as Lab3 and HomeRepair, treat it as narrative because it carries the
offshore/global-teams requirement. **2sol is well outside the cutline but carries the single most
important evidence in this application** (vendor-to-client, concurrent projects, single point of
contact), so it gets narrative treatment as a deliberate exception - state this so Alan can move it.
Everything else stays as plain bullets or folds into Earlier.

Nagare: **leave it out.** Tone caution in [[positioning]] puts it on the "lead with it" side for
product/EM roles, but this is a client-services PM role where the reader wants client focus. Neutral
at best here.
