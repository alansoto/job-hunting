# JD Analysis - Concentrix Catalyst, Project Manager

**Primary pain point.** Catalyst sells digital delivery to clients. They need a PM who can hold a
client account on their own: several projects running at once for the same client, the client
calling one number, and a mixed team of designers, engineers, and analysts producing work that
justifies the fee. The underlying need is account-level trust, not project administration.

**Top 3 priorities.** (1) Multiple concurrent projects for one client, with the PM as single point
of contact. (2) Leading diverse cross-functional teams, UX/UI plus engineers plus analysts. (3)
Digital/software delivery expertise, with Native Mobile called out specifically.

**Culture signals.** "Forward-thinking", "thrives on solving complex challenges", "be a catalyst for
change", free lunch Thursdays. Consultancy energy: confident, a little polished, not corporate-stiff
and not scale-up casual. Prose paragraphs will land better than a bullet block here, and the letter
should sound like someone comfortable in front of a client.

**Best hook angle.** 2sol. Five years as Flight Centre's delivery partner, responsible for every
project on a $5M-a-month platform, the client's main contact, a team of five, 10+ products, 20+
APIs. That is this job, one employer earlier. Open there, directly, no throat-clearing.

**Gap trigger.** Yes, one: Native Mobile. Named as an expertise requirement and genuinely unmet.
Handle it with the capability-hierarchy reframe and do not stretch the BMA mobile learning content
into a native app claim. Smartsheet is not worth raising.
